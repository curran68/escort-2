MK2 Escort a brief history

The Mark II Escort was on sale between 1975 and 1980. The first models were rolled off the production lines on 2 December 1974. Unlike the MK1 escort, the MK2 was developed along withÂ Ford of Germany. This was codenamed Brenda during its development, with the same mechanicals as the MK1. The 950cc engine was the lowest engined variation. The estate and van versions used the same panelwork as the MK1, but with the MK2 front end and interior. The car used a revised underbody, which had in fact been introduced as a running change during the last six months of the life of the MK1. This car made a point, although there was varied base models, in this website I have only covered the RS Mexico, RS 1800 and RS2000 which were aimed at the performance market. During the second half of the 1970s, the Escort continued to prove hugely popular with buyers in Britain and other parts of Europe. In 1979 and 1980 three special edition Escorts were launched: the Linnet, Harrier and Goldcrest. After an incredibly popular model run, the MK2 ended in Britain in August 1980, other countries following soon after.
